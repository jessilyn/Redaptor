What is the R-Index?

The reuse-index (R-Index) is a metric that quantifies the level of reusability of a published text. It is automatically computed and then manually refined. It is based on identifying research components like datasets, algorithms, protocols, and statistics tools. Components are reproducible if they are publicly available and/or all the information necessary to reproduce the published results is present in the paper. This metric can be aggregated at the author, journal, and institutional level by accounting for the reuse­index of all articles in each respective pool.

How is it calculated?

Our current demo shows two R-Indices calculated by expected versus included details in Methods sections. [more]

Our current demo shows two R-Indices calculated by expected versus included details in Methods sections. More specifically, here, we analyze four overarching categories as shown in the flow chart below (Figure 1). Those four categories were further broken (see Table 1 below) down in an individualized manner to each article to determine whether the Methods section content is sufficient for reproducing that work. In the future, the R-Index will be calculated using the DeepDive platform by comparing the content of an individual paper to the entire corpus of literature. Using natural language processing (NLP), DeepDive will “learn” what content should be included in an article based on common co-occurrences of subcontent in the entire body of scientific literature. This R-Index will be further refined by crowdsourcing, and feedback from the scientific community will refine the distance supervision rules which will enhance the reuse index algorithm.
